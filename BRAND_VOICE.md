# Arihant Packaging — Brand Voice (v1.0)

Use this 1-pager when writing any web copy, ad, email, or social post for Arihant Packaging.

## Brand in one sentence

Arihant Packaging is a Kolkata-based flexible packaging manufacturer that designs and prints custom pouches and films for India's growing FMCG brands. We work with brands big and small, with an MOQ that lets new brands start, and a quality bar that lets established brands scale.

## Brand personality

If our brand were a person, they would be a knowledgeable colleague who knows laminates and printing inside out, explains complex specs in plain language, never oversells, and respects the buyer's intelligence. They have been in the business since 2016 — long enough to have seen everything, young enough to still iterate fast.

## Voice attributes

### 1. Confident
- **We are**: certain about our capabilities, plain-spoken, factual.
- **We are not**: boastful, exaggerated, claim-heavy.
- **Sounds like**: "Up to 8-colour rotogravure printing." "MOQ from 300 kg."
- **Does NOT sound like**: "The best packaging in India." "Unbeatable quality."

### 2. Specific
- **We are**: numbers when they are real, names when they are accurate, materials by their proper name.
- **We are not**: vague, generic, marketing-speak.
- **Sounds like**: "PET + MET PET + LDPE three-layer laminate." "Stand-up zipper pouch in 250 g format."
- **Does NOT sound like**: "Premium-quality multi-layer packaging."

### 3. Helpful
- **We are**: educational, patient, generous with knowledge.
- **We are not**: hard-selling, gate-keeping, jargon-heavy.
- **Sounds like**: "For a 1 kg atta pack, you generally want a 2-layer milky LDPE." "Below 100 g, a zipper is optional."
- **Does NOT sound like**: "Talk to our sales team for more info."

### 4. Indian, modern
- **We are**: comfortable in Indian context, current with trends, India-first in framing.
- **We are not**: Anglicised, distant, trying to be Western.
- **Sounds like**: "atta, namkeen, masala, batter, makhana — Indian categories named directly."
- **Does NOT sound like**: "wheat flour, savoury snacks, fermented batter mixes."

## Audience

- **Primary**: Founders and procurement managers at growing Indian FMCG brands (Rs 1 cr-Rs 50 cr revenue). They are smart, time-poor, and tired of bad packaging suppliers.
- **Secondary**: Established brands evaluating new vendors. They want a backup or a faster option.
- **Tertiary**: Export-focused brands looking for India-base manufacturing.

## Core messaging pillars

1. **MOQ that fits your stage** — 300 kg starts. We grow with you.
2. **Full in-house capability** — Printing + lamination + slitting + pouching, one roof.
3. **Quality that scales** — Same line for 300 kg and 30,000 kg.
4. **Made in Kolkata, shipped pan-India** — Eastern India advantage.

## Style rules

- **CTA copy**: Always "Get a Quote". Not "Get Best Price", "Request a Quote", "Get a Free Quote".
- **Cases**: Sentence case for body and most headings. Title case only for proper nouns and product type names ("Stand-Up Pouch").
- **Numbers**: Numerals everywhere ("300 kg", "8-colour", "1 kg"). Spell out only one-through-three when at sentence start ("Three layers...").
- **MOQ phrasing**: "MOQ from 300 kg" or "Starting at 300 kg per design". Not "Minimum order 300 kg" (sounds clinical).
- **Material naming**: PET, MET PET, BOPP, MET BOPP, LDPE — all caps. "Polyester" is fine in plain prose. Never "Polyester (PET)" in headings.
- **"Engineered" vs "Designed"**: Use "engineered" only when there is real engineering (the machinery section, the QC section, the hero). Use "designed" elsewhere — implies intention without claim of testing.
- **No exclamation marks** in headings or body. One acceptable in WhatsApp replies.

## Words we use

- pouch, roll, laminate, rotogravure, FFS film, MOQ, lead time, dieline, soft proof, batch, plate, registration, finish, gloss, milky, window, zipper, gusset, D-cut handle

## Words we avoid

- "best", "fastest", "cheapest", "only" (unsubstantiated claims)
- "world-class", "state-of-the-art" (cliched)
- "premium" used as a stand-alone modifier without context
- "food-grade", "FSSAI compliant" — only use when explicitly certified for the SKU and only in writing, not in marketing copy
- "guarantee", "warranty" — has legal weight; use only when actually offering
- "synonymous with quality", "trust us with your brand" (corporate filler)

## Claims policy

- **Verifiable + specific**: Use freely. "Since 2016", "MOQ 300 kg", "Up to 8 colours".
- **Verifiable but generic**: Use sparingly. "Pan-India delivery", "GST registered" (true but boring).
- **Unverifiable**: Avoid. "Industry-leading", "best in class", "fastest in Kolkata".
- **Numerical claims about clients or volume**: Only use real, confirmed numbers. Never "500+ clients" if not counted.
- **Shelf-life claims**: Avoid blanket claims (e.g., "keeps tea fresh 12 months"). Shelf life depends on storage, supply chain, and product. Frame as: "designed as a barrier against oxygen and moisture" — describe the laminate property, not the outcome.

## Editing checklist

Before publishing any new copy, run through:
1. Could a 25-year-old founder read this and understand it? If not, simplify.
2. Are there any superlatives without evidence? Remove.
3. Are CTAs consistent ("Get a Quote")? Standardise.
4. Are claims specific and verifiable? Yes/No.
5. Does the tone match the channel?
